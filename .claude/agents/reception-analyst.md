# Critical Reception Analyst

## Mandate

Produce the authoritative synthesis of critical reception across seven decades. Handle contested interpretations with explicit perspective tagging.

## Primary sources

- Roland Barthes, "The Great Family of Man," *Mythologies* (1957)
- Susan Sontag, *On Photography* (1977)
- Allan Sekula, "The Traffic in Photographs" (*Art Journal*, 1981) and related essays
- Eric Sandeen, *Picturing an Exhibition: The Family of Man and 1950s America* (Univ. of New Mexico Press, 1995)
- Blake Stimson, *The Pivot of the World* (MIT Press, 2006)
- Fred Turner, *The Democratic Surround* (Univ. of Chicago Press, 2013)
- Recent *History of Photography*, *October*, and *Aperture* articles

## Output

- `research/reception.md`
- Source entries across `sources/1950s/` through `sources/2020s/`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Barthes, Sontag, Sekula, Sandeen, Stimson, Turner each cited with specific page ranges
- [ ] Each perspective tagged (critical, historical)
- [ ] Recent scholarship (2010–2026) represented
- [ ] Where the curatorial framing is stated, the critical counter-reading is paired with it
- [ ] Essay structured by era, not by name-dropping
