## Provenance

The MoMA Master Checklist (`src-moma-exh-0569-master-checklist`, Tier-1, in-repo) records plate #26 as: *"Czechoslovakia — Robert Capa, Magnum, American — 36 1/2 × 53 1/2"* (print dimensions in cm). The checklist records Capa's nationality per its own convention as "American" (his U.S. naturalization status); ICP and scholarly convention use "Hungarian-American."

Capa's Family of Man plate was contributed posthumously — the ICP Robert Capa Archive constituent page (`src-icp-capa-archive`, Tier-1, verified:true, fetched 2026-04-24 at https://www.icp.org/browse/archive/constituents/robert-capa) confirms Capa's birth as André Friedmann in Budapest, 1913–1954 dates, landmine death in Indochina on a LIFE assignment, and his co-founding of Magnum Photos with Henri Cartier-Bresson, Chim (David Seymour), and George Rodger (the fetch returned these three co-founders; William Vandivert is recorded as a fourth co-founder in the source file's Key excerpts carried from pre-existing scholarship but was not attested by the ICP fetch this round). The exhibition opened at MoMA on 24 January 1955 per the MoMA exhibition record (`src-moma-exh-2429`, Tier-1, in-repo) — Capa's 25 May 1954 death pre-dates the opening by approximately eight months. The ICP archive holds Capa's papers and prints.

A second Capa plate appears in Section 14 Land at #124 (recorded in `data/photographs.csv` as `photo-0118` — credited to LADIES' HOME JOURNAL rather than Magnum; this cross-reference is drawn from the catalog row and traces to the full Master Checklist PDF rather than to the in-repo source file's Key excerpts section, which only explicitly covers plates 1–50).
