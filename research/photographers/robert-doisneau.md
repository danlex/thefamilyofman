# Robert Doisneau (1912–1994)

Robert Doisneau was a French photographer — a central figure of the postwar French humanist school — whose everyday Paris street photography made him one of the most recognized photographers of the twentieth century (src-lemonde-1994-doisneau-obit). Born in Gentilly (Seine) on 14 April 1912 and died in Montrouge on 1 April 1994, he worked for the Rapho agency (Rapho Guillumette) for most of his career; in the MoMA Master Checklist for *The Family of Man*, his three plates are each attributed via Rapho (src-moma-exh-0569-master-checklist).

## Career

Doisneau's professional identity crystallised in the late 1930s through the 1950s around images of Parisian neighborhoods, schoolchildren, cafés, lovers, and working-class leisure — a body of work that Le Monde described in its 1994 obituary as "photographe de la vie quotidienne" (src-lemonde-1994-doisneau-obit). The 1950 photograph *Le Baiser de l'Hôtel de Ville* ("The Kiss by the Hôtel de Ville"), originally an assignment for *LIFE*, became his most-reproduced image — though court proceedings in the 1990s established that the couple shown had been informally posed rather than candid.

## Connection to *The Family of Man*

Doisneau is represented by three plates in the *Family of Man* Master Checklist, all clustered in Section 2 "Lovers": #14 (France), #16 (France), and #20 (France) (src-moma-exh-0569-master-checklist). Each is credited "Robert Doisneau, Rapho Guillumette, French" in the checklist and appears untitled and undated — consistent with Steichen's editorial practice of stripping titles and dates from all 503 plates so that the images would be read as one continuous argument about human commonality (see `src-cna-education`).

Whether any of the three plates is identifiable as *Le Baiser de l'Hôtel de Ville* cannot be resolved from the checklist itself and is left unconfirmed in `data/photographs.csv` (see notes on photo-0011).
