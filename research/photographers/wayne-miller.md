# Wayne Miller (1918–2013)

Wayne F. Miller was an American photographer and Magnum member who served as Edward Steichen's curatorial assistant on *The Family of Man* (src-nyt-2013-wayne-miller-obit; src-magnum-photographer-bios). Born in Chicago on 19 September 1918 and trained under Steichen in the US Navy's Aviation Photographic Unit during the Second World War, Miller is represented in the MoMA Master Checklist by at least six prints in the first 47 plates alone (#22, #29, #42, #43, #44, #47) — more than any other contributor in that range (src-moma-exh-0569-master-checklist). He died in Orinda, California on 22 May 2013 (src-nyt-2013-wayne-miller-obit).

## Career

Miller's early independent project was *Chicago's South Side, 1946–1948* (published book: University of California Press, 2000) — a Guggenheim-funded documentary series on African-American life in postwar Chicago. After his curatorial work on *The Family of Man* he joined Magnum Photos as a full member and served as Magnum's president from 1962 to 1966 (src-magnum-photographer-bios).

## Connection to *The Family of Man*

Miller's role on the exhibition was substantial. He and Steichen together screened hundreds of thousands of submissions through 1953–54, and Miller's own plates are concentrated in the Childbirth section (plates #42–#44) — widely reported in secondary sources as documenting the 1946 birth of Miller's own son, David Miller. Under Caption 4 of the exhibition, installed beneath plate #44, reads the Scriabin line "The universe resounds with the joyful cry *I am*…" (src-moma-exh-0569-master-checklist). Our `data/photographs.csv` does not assert the Miller-family provenance of these plates at the row level without a Tier-1 confirmation; Miller's own later writings and the Magnum biography both describe the sequence this way.

The deluxe edition of the 1955 catalog (Simon and Schuster / Maco / MoMA) carries "photographic footnotes" by Miller documenting the exhibition's installation (src-moma-1955-catalog).
